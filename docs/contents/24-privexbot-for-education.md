# PrivexBot for Education: Student and Staff Support

## Introduction

Educational institutions run on information. Prospective students want to know about programs. Current students need help with registration. Parents have questions about tuition. Staff need IT support.

The challenge: limited resources, unlimited questions, and peak periods that overwhelm even the best-staffed offices.

This guide shows how schools, colleges, universities, and training organizations can use AI chatbots to serve their communities better—students, parents, faculty, and staff.

### IT Bot Interactions

```
Password Reset:

User: "I forgot my password"

Bot: "No problem! Here's how to reset your password:

**Self-Service Reset:**
1. Go to password.institution.edu
2. Enter your username (usually your student ID)
3. Verify your identity (phone or email)
4. Create a new password

**Password Requirements:**
- At least 12 characters
- Mix of upper/lower case, numbers, symbols
- Can't be one of your last 10 passwords

**If that doesn't work:**
- Visit the IT Help Desk in Library 101
- Bring your student ID for verification
- Hours: Mon-Fri 8am-8pm, Sat 10am-4pm

Is this for your student account, email, or a
specific system?"

---

WiFi Troubleshooting:

User: "WiFi isn't working"

Bot: "Let's troubleshoot your WiFi connection:

**Quick Fixes:**
1. Forget the network and reconnect
2. Restart your device
3. Make sure you're connecting to 'Institution-Secure'
   (not 'Institution-Guest')

**Connecting to Institution-Secure:**
1. Select 'Institution-Secure' from WiFi networks
2. Username: your student email
3. Password: your portal password

**Still not working?**
Check our status page: status.institution.edu

**Current Status:** ✓ All systems operational

If problems persist, bring your device to IT Help Desk
(Library 101) for in-person assistance.

What device are you using? (This might help narrow it down)"
```

### Parent Communication

```
FERPA-Aware Response:

User: "My son won't tell me his grades. Can you tell me?"

Bot: "I understand that can be frustrating. Due to FERPA
(federal privacy law), we cannot share academic records
with anyone—including parents—without the student's
written consent, once they turn 18.

**Options:**
1. **FERPA Release:** Your student can sign a FERPA
   release form allowing us to share information with you.
   Form at registrar.institution.edu/ferpa-release

2. **Student Portal:** Students can give you view-only
   access to their portal, which shows grades.

3. **Conversation:** We encourage open communication.
   Our Family Resources page has tips for having these
   conversations: family.institution.edu

**We're Here to Help:**
If you have concerns about your student's wellbeing,
our Dean of Students office can do a welfare check:
(555) 123-4567.

Is there other information I can help you with?"
```

### FERPA Considerations

```
FERPA Compliance:

PROTECTED INFORMATION:
├── Grades and transcripts
├── Enrollment status
├── Class schedules
├── Financial records
├── Disciplinary records
└── Anything in student record

BOT CONFIGURATION:
├── Never display student-specific data
├── Direct personal inquiries to secure channels
├── Explain FERPA when parents ask
├── No student record lookups
└── General information only

SAFE TOPICS:
├── General policies (apply to everyone)
├── Public directory information (if student allows)
├── Process information (how to do things)
├── Contact information (offices, not individuals)
└── Deadlines and calendars
```
